# Electromechanical Evolution and Visual Documentation
The following visual documentation provides a comprehensive, six-axis view of the Piolín electromechanical architecture across its primary developmental phases. For the WRO Future Engineers competition, transparent engineering documentation is critical. These orthographic projections (Front, Back, Left, Right, Top, and Bottom) serve as the official physical record of our chassis evolution, detailing how component placement, center of gravity, and structural rigidity were optimized over time.

We maintain these specific visual iterations to illustrate our hardware design methodology. The grids below map our transition from a heavily constrained baseline model ("COMPLETE LEGO") to an intermediate testing prototype ("PARTIALLY LEGO"), and finally to our competition-ready hybrid platform ("V3 LEGO"). By comparing these phases side-by-side, the structural improvements and spatial planning become immediately apparent. These images document the strategic integration of the EV3 Intelligent Brick, the secure mounting of the PixyCam 2.1, and the positioning of our ultrasonic sensor array, while also highlighting our deliberate cable management routing.

Crucially, these views track our mechanical solutions for minimizing chassis flex and maximizing steering stability. The progression into the V3 architecture visually demonstrates the shift toward a rigid, cross-braced frame. It captures the integration of custom 3D-printed bevel gears and the corrected steering knuckle assembly—specifically highlighting the implementation of the essential 3-hole Technic pieces that eliminated the torsional twisting of earlier phases. Ultimately, these blueprints validate that Piolín is a meticulously engineered platform built for high-speed autonomous navigation.
