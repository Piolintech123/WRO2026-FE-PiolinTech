## Project History & Timeline

Our journey began in June 2025. What started as an ambitious project quickly became a series of engineering trials, hardware disasters, and constant redesigns. This timeline documents our path from the initial prototypes to the competitive, high-performance robot we field today.

### 2025: The Foundation and The Failures

*   **June 2025: The Beginning**
    We started with our first custom robot version, fueled by the excitement of entering the WRO arena. At this stage, we were focused on understanding basic motor control and structural integrity.
    <p align="center"><img src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e8f6a7bf-9663-4bfb-8b8d-1fb598d62b1d" width="200"></p>

*   **July 2025: The Yahboom Fire**
    Disaster struck when our Yahboom kit suffered a catastrophic hardware failure and caught fire. This event left us at a complete standstill, forcing us to rethink our entire hardware procurement and safety strategy.
    <p align="center"><img src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c2c7ec54-d42e-4f9c-9a4a-a2b69047f0b2" width="200"></p>

*   **August 2025: The "Against Rules" Prototype**
    To keep our development momentum alive, we built an experimental robot featuring one motor, two wheels, and a spherical support. While highly functional in terms of movement, it was strictly non-compliant with WRO regulations. This taught us that engineering capability must always be balanced with strict rule adherence.
    <p align="center"><img src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c0980ce3-dff4-45dd-8280-052b609c39e0" width="200"></p>

*   **Late 2025: The "Forbidden" v1**
    We developed a new, more robust design that was mechanically sound, but it suffered from extremely slow performance. We nicknamed it the "Forbidden" prototype. It was a clear signal that we needed to move toward more efficient propulsion systems.
    <p align="center"><img src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b472c9dc-02f2-445b-b1bf-cff487a16ce0" width="200"></p>

*   **End of 2025: Nationals & Americas**
    Despite the setbacks, our persistence paid off. We successfully iterated through multiple chassis designs to reach the WRO Nationals and the Americas stage. These competitions provided the real-world data and feedback needed to transition from amateur building to competitive robotics.
    <p align="center"><img src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b02d076a-0c1f-44d6-8297-e713f1c7f70d" width="200"></p>

### 2026: The Hybrid Era

*   2026: Piolín Development
Entering 2026, we finalized the architecture of "Piolín" specifically for the WRO Future Engineers competition. We transitioned to the LEGO Mindstorms EV3 platform to leverage its deterministic, integrated performance and robust handling of sensor data. Our chassis design evolved into a hybrid system, combining the modularity of LEGO Technic with custom 3D-printed structural components to ensure maximum rigidity and steering precision. This iteration focuses on high-level reliability, stability, and consistent autonomous navigation, providing a proven, competition-ready foundation.
